# Naming the Ratio Perp DEX — SHEAR

> The product was prototyped under the working name VERSUS; the locked name is **SHEAR**. The word *is* the product: shear is the relative motion between two layers under stress — exactly what a ratio perp trades.

## Step 0 — Layers (separate, don't merge)

- **Product truth:** A perp DEX where the instrument is the *ratio* between two assets. One position, one margin, one liquidation, zero leg drift. Long `SOL-ETH` profits when SOL outperforms ETH, regardless of market direction.
- **Brand promise:** Trade the relationship, not the direction. Quant-grade relative-value primitives that retail can touch.
- **Name job:** Read as a serious financial-instrument primitive — and sit cleanly next to its siblings SHIM and SLIP as one product family.

## Step 1 — Constraints (hard rules)

- 1 syllable, matching the SHIM / SLIP cadence.
- Starts with S + consonant cluster (SH, SL, SP, SK) so the names form a family wordmark.
- Hard ending (M, P, R, T, K) for graphic weight.
- Doubles as a real word with both **mechanical** and **financial** meaning (a shim wedges two surfaces; a slip is dock paperwork / price drift; a *shear* is relative motion between two layers).
- Must clear search against crypto venues, especially Solana and Hyperliquid.
- Must survive the "is this gambling?" sniff test — the prize is a *trading* build, so the name can't lean casino. (This is where the old working name VERSUS was weakest: "A vs B" read as a duel/bet. SHEAR reads as engineering + quant finance.)

## Step 2 — Enemy

"We are not for legged pair trades, two-margin headaches, or directional gambling dressed up as relative value." If a candidate feels neutral to that, it fails the Signal test.

## Why SHEAR (the case)

**Mechanical metaphor.** Shear stress is the force two layers exert on each other when sliding past each other under load — the classic engineering diagram is two surfaces with an arrow showing relative motion. That is exactly what a ratio perp trades: the relative motion between two assets.

**Financial metaphor.** "Vol-surface shear" is the working term quants use for the difference in implied vol across strikes/expiries — the geometry of risk between paired instruments. The financial vocabulary already names the thing.

**Reframes correctly, without the gambling tone.** The old working name VERSUS framed it as "A vs B," a duel — memorable but it read as a bet. SHEAR reframes the *same* mechanic in engineering/quant language: you trade the shear between two assets. Serious, ownable, and it removes the only real risk VERSUS carried for a "pure trading" prize.

**Systemically.** Shear Pro, Shear Pools, Shear Labs, Shear Index — every sub-brand inherits the root. The wordmark survives at a 32×32 favicon (anchor on H and R).

## Origin stories (locked artifacts)

**10-second:** "Shear is the relative motion between two layers. We made that motion tradeable — one position, one margin, one liquidation."

**30-second:** "In mechanics, shear is what happens when two surfaces move against each other under stress. In markets, the same thing happens every day between every pair of assets. Quants have traded that motion for forty years using two positions and two margin accounts. SHEAR collapses it into a single perp on the relationship itself — one instrument, one liquidation, zero leg drift. The name nods to the engineers who named the phenomenon and the quants who built careers trading it."
